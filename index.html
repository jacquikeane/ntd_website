What are NTDs and why are we interested in them?

Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTDs) are a diverse group of infectious diseases caused by pathogens, including parasitic worms, protozoa, bacteria and viruses. The diseases affect over 1 billion people across the world. NTDs mostly affect people living in regions of poverty and poor sanitation in tropical and subtropical regions. The infections are not as deadly as some other diseases, such as malaria, HIV and tuberculosis, but they do cause a wide range of health problems that in many cases have added social and economic implications.

Who are we and what are we doing about NTDs?

We are a group of scientists from the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute who are researching the biology of the parasites and bacteria that cause NTDs.  We want to find out how they spread and how they interact with us and our tissues. We are doing this by studying their genomes. We hope that our research will help towards reducing NTDs: either by identifying weaknesses in the pathogens’ armour that can be exploited with drugs and vaccines, or simply by understanding more about how people can avoid becoming infected.
